    Attention Important Information

    Updated 4-3-2020

    From Kelly Krostag, Coordinator of Human Resources

    Due to executive orders by Governor Ron Desantis requiring districts to implement an Instructional Continuity Plan remotely, all requirements of the performance evaluation are now waived for 2019 - 2020 school year.

    The 90 Day Process that is the result of an overall evaluation of Unsatisfactory will be waived for both instructional and educational support units because of the inability to complete evaluations by the end of the school year and/or add a metric of student performance.

    Just to clarify, you are not required to complete your PDP or have a second observation. We realize that most teachers used FSA or EOC tests as their data points, so those won't be available to complete the PDP.

     

    Additional Clarification:

    Teachers in the START Program will still complete the process, but you will not have any new required observations. Principals will still fill in the Domain 4 information for the teachers in the START Program.

    Frequently Asked Questions with Answers:
    Q1: "Does my first observation become my final score?"

    A1: You will not have a final score because we will not be finalizing the

    evaluations.

     

    Q2: What about my bonus for Best and Brightest since I can't finish my evaluation?

    A2: The legislature did not renew the Best and Brightest legislation in the last legislative session, so there was never going to be a bonus for Best and Brightest next year.

     

    Q3: Should I finish my PDP?

    A3: No, you do not need to finish your PDP because there is no data point available.

     

    Q4: So we will not have an evaluation score this year?

    A4: No. We will not have a final evaluation score this year. The law requires three metrics for a complete evaluation, and we were not going to have student data. We waived the evaluations with agreement from both unions.

     

    Q5: I am in START. I know I will finish the program but my score won't count for performance pay because we won't have that next year, but will I receive my START Certificate if I successfully complete the program?

    A5: Yes. We will get your certificates to you.

    Performance Matters Name Change

    The District's Evaluation Management System and Professional Learning Management System formerly known as Performance Matters is now referred to as TalentED.  Our Evaluation and Professional Learning Management System is now owned by PowerSchools.  We have seen several name changes of the past few years due to companies buying out each other.  For consistency purpose we will as a district refer to the online resource as CORE-Eval and CORE-PD.

    From a functional standpoint, nothing has changed other than the name.

    3. Overall effectiveness rating Updated: 11-2-17 

    An educator's overall effectiveness rating is the average of the two parts of the evaluation: the professional practices and student learning growth portions. For each portion of the evaluation, the rating achieved corresponds to a number (4, 3, 2, 1) that is used to calculate the sum total of the two halves of the evaluation, to yield a final, summative rating, as shown below:

    For example, an employee with a: Updated: 4-25-16 
    Highly Effective Employee Appraisal Framework Score (4 points), and an Highly Effective PDP Score, and an Effective Student Growth Score (3 points)
    would be calculated as follows: (4*0.65) + (4*0.02) + (3*0.33) =   3.67 points for a summative rating of Highly Effective. 

     

    Professional Practice 65%
    PDP 2%
    Student Growth 33%
    Final Summative Rating 100%
    4 x 0.65 = 2.60
    4 x 0.02 = 0.08
    3 x 0.33 = 0.99
    3.67 = Highly Effective
